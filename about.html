In the ancient days of darkness and fire-- before the oceans drank Atlantis; before the earth cracked and like, lots of lava came up and totally made everything glow red and it was pretty hardcore; like at least a year ago-- two heroes were called upon to cover for their ailing uncle and host his public radio program: Uncle Mathison's Family Radio Hour. Those heroes, clad in the armor of Slayer and wielding the weapons of rock, were the cousins: Kenneth and Drew Mathison. As they valiantly struggled to navigate the most un-metal like waters of public radio they discovered that the radio station had evil plans to replace their uncle's show with a sports program! In desperation, and aided by the masterful sound-man abilities of their new friend Femur, the two boys created Death Metal Family Radio Hour, the most hardcore hour of radio that has ever happened. Like, dudes, we're not even kidding. It was hardcore. Ultimately they failed to save their uncle's show, but at the eleventh hour, they picked up an awesome contract to do a podcast for kids! And in shadowed corners throughout the land, where men dwell in fear under darkened skies, people are totally still whispering this story and wondering when... if ever... the heroes will return to totally rock out.

About IndyFringe

IndyFringe is a place, an event, an environment, an attitude, a movement, an incubator for new talent and a magnet for imaginative, thoughtful, hopeful and transformational people. IndyFringe is also an annual Theatre Festival and a year round theatre for emerging artists and new work. The 11-day performing arts festival is held on 8 stages in five venues in and around Massachusetts Avenue and at The Cook Theatre at Indiana Landmarks Center in Indianapolis. IndyFringe will celebrate its l0th Anniversary in 2014.

About Bloomington Playwrights Project

The Bloomington Playwrights Project (BPP) is the only professional theatre in the entire state of Indiana dedicated solely to new plays. In operation for 34 years, BPP serves both the Bloomington and national theatre community by working with talented playwrights, actors, and artists to develop high-level productions for our patrons as well as the many audiences attending subsequent productions of scripts that originated at the BPP. BPP is an advocate for playwrights and new plays and believe firmly that they are not only important but absolutely vital to the future of our art form.
